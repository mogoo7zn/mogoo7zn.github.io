
^Safety is one of the core pillars of our iGEM project. We were committed to ensuring the safety of our team members, the environment, and the public throughout every stage of our project—from experimental design to the entire project’ s implementation. Our safety strategy was built on three main components: Biosafety, Wet Lab Safety and Human Practices Safety. Here, we outlined the specific measures we have taken in each component.^

# 1. Biosafety

    To prevent the accidental release of genetically modified organisms (GMOs) into the environment, we designed a robust biosafety system that ensures our engineered strains remain confined to the laboratory.

+ ^1.1 Arabinose-repressed Suicide System^

    A central concern in biosafety is how to keep the strains from leaking out of the reaction system. For prevention of improper leakage, the strain we constructed could survive exclusively within the detection system (an enclosed detection device), we designed the arabinose-repressed suicide system of engineered strains through a constitutively expressed suicide switch.

    The system included an activator-based switch Lex operon (LexO), a repressor-based switch Ara operon (AraO), Arabinose Repressor (AraR), Tetracycline Repressor (TetR) and K1 killer toxin (K1). When arabinose is present in the system, it binds to AraRA thereby promoting AraRA’ s binding to LexO, which helps activate the transcription of downstream genes. Arabinose also binds to AraRR, but conversely its binding inhibits AraRR’ s binding to AraO, thus relieving the repression of transcription$^1$. Briefly, if the system contains enough arabinose, the TetR, which binded to *PtetO1* (the operator sequence of TetR) will be expressed and inhibits the transcription of downstream genes$^2$. Then the K1 will not be expressed and the strains can survive normally. When outside the system with no arabinose, the suicide switch would be turned on and K1 will be expressed, thereby disrupting the strains’ ion homeostasis and membrane potential$^3$, and as a result, the strains will be killed by the suicide system. (Figure 1)

    ![Figure 1 The Arabinose-repressed Suicide System.]( https://static.igem.wiki/teams/5924/safety/fig1.webp)

    Thanks to the exogenous nature of the system components and high affinity of the operon for the operator sequence, this suicide system exhibits a very low escape rate$^1$. Moreover, the inducer of this system, arabinose, has very extensive sources (including various agricultural products and natural plants), which makes it more affordable as an inducer in comparison with galactose, tryptophan and etc. And for K1, it exhibits strong killing activity against yeast in slightly acidic environment, which matches the growth conditions of yeasts$^3$. And this toxin is non-pathogenic to mammalian cells$^4$, making this system more biosafe. 

+ ^1.2 Auxotrophic Chassis Strain^

    We chose *S. cerevisiae* BY4741 *ura3*Δ，*his3*Δ, *met15*Δ, *leu2*Δ as the chassis organism. Although the strains were complemented of auxotrophic deficiency in a degree due to the introduction of the marker gene, the two strains retained methionine auxotrophy. And for this, they can’t survive outside the detection device in general, making the accidental leakage less possible.

# 2. Wet Lab Safety

    The wet lab safety is equally important as safety design in the project. The main objective of the wet lab safety is to protect the lab staff, biological samples, and the environment. In this case, proper personal training, strict laboratory management and reliable safety facilities are necessary.

+ ^2.1 Personnel Training^

    One of the most important issues of laboratory safety is to equip laboratory personnel with standardized experimental skills and comprehensive understanding of laboratory safety. Each of our team members must pass two assessments: completing virtual simulation experiments and finishing on-site training.

    Virtual simulation experiment: lab members have practiced virtual experiment operations and learned laboratory management regulations on the virtual simulation experiment platform.

    ![Virtual simulation experiment platment.]( https://static.igem.wiki/teams/5924/safety/psfig1.webp)

    On-site Training: Hands-on training, where basic experiment operations were taught hands-on, is conducted by experienced members to inexperienced ones, ensuring proficiency in aseptic techniques and proper handling of biological materials. 

+ ^2.2 Laboratory Safety Equipment^

    Our lab is well-equipped with safety facilities to respond promptly to emergencies and maintain a safe working environment:

    Emergency Equipment: Eye wash stations, fire blankets, and window breakers are installed for rapid response to chemical exposure or fire hazards.

+ ^2.3 Reagent Management^

According to the Regulation on the Administration of Biosafety of Pathogenic Microbiology Laboratories—China’s foundational statute for laboratory biosafety management—in vivo active botulinum toxin A(BoNT/A) may be handled only in a BSL-3 (or higher) facility to guarantee personnel safety. We therefore employed TEV protease to simulate the cleavage of the fusion protein Pir1p-SNAP25-(TEV)-α-factor by BoNT/A.

Our lab is also equipped reagent cabinets that can control temperature and humidity, while ensuring the cleanliness and orderliness of the laboratory for a long time. In particular, volatile reagents are kept in the fume hood.

# 3. Human Practices Safety

+ ^3.1 Personal Safety^

    Before entering the laboratory and participating in activities such as bacteria painting, visitors are educated about laboratory safety rules. We required everyone to wear personal protective equipment, including masks, gloves, and lab coats. When introducing the wonders of microorganisms to children with autism, we organized a science education session specifically designed for them. All experimental materials used were safe and commonly encountered in daily life. Furthermore, each child was accompanied by a dedicated volunteer to ensure their safety throughout the activity.

+ ^3.2 Personal Privacy Safety^

    Throughout our human practices, protecting participants' personal privacy and individual rights was of paramount importance. Specifically, we adopted an anonymous approach in our questionnaires and assured participants that the collected data would be used solely for research purposes. For any interviews requiring audio or video recording, we obtained explicit prior consent from the interviewees. Additionally, we sought advance approval from the interviewees before publishing any interview content online. In summary, we took comprehensive measures to safeguard the personal privacy of all participants.
